§ problem

Markets are noisy. They are not structureless.

This project asks whether sentiment and price behavior contain useful temporal patterns. Especially short-horizon dependencies and cyclical structure that yield to spectral inspection. The goal isn't a magic prediction score. It's a careful, well-instrumented look at what kind of signal is actually present in a window of equity data.

§ data + features

What feeds the model.

  • Equity price data for NVDA, AMD, TSM, INTC, AVGO.
  • Financial news sentiment features built from text sources.
  • Lagged sentiment, returns, volatility, and engineered time-series predictors.
  • Rolling-window features to keep training honest about look-ahead.
§ methods

Where the math lives.

  • Periodograms to inspect frequency power and possible cycles.
  • Autoregressive (AR) models to measure temporal dependence.
  • Feature engineering for short-horizon predictive modeling.
  • LLM interpretation layer using LoRA / QLoRA-style fine-tuning ideas.
§ why it matters

The thing I actually like about this project.

It sits at the intersection of two things I keep being pulled toward: mathematical signal analysis and concrete financial interpretation. The output is not just a score. It's a system that can explain, in plain language, what structure it thinks it found, which is a much harder claim to defend than "the AUC went up".

§ stack

Tooling.

Python, pandas, NumPy, statsmodels, spectral analysis, AR models, sentiment engineering, and LLM-based interpretation.
