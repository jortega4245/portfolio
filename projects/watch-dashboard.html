§ live deployment

This one is shipped — go look at it.

The dashboard is built for exploration, not static reporting. Users can filter, compare, and inspect patterns across brand, model, movement, and price behavior. It's published as an interactive R Shiny app and lives on GitHub Pages alongside the rest of the portfolio.

§ problem

What actually drives a watch's price.

Watch prices are driven by brand, model, movement type, case material, condition, scarcity, and metadata. The project turns a curated collection of listings into a single interactive surface for asking value-driver questions — and an exercise in shipping something past the notebook.

§ analysis

Methods, briefly.

  • Regression to study price determinants and brand premia.
  • Clustering to group similar listings and model families.
  • Text mining on model names, descriptions, and metadata.
  • Interactive visualization through R Shiny — filters, comparisons, and brand drill-downs.
§ what it shows

Beyond "I trained a model".

This project is the part of my work I'm most attached to — building data products, not just models. Running a regression is one skill. Building something a non-technical person can actually explore is a different one, and that's the one I wanted to practice.
