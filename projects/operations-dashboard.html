Operations & KPI dashboards.

Six years on a large logistics account: workforce optimization, scheduling, payroll accuracy, and KPI reporting that has to survive the daily ops standup, not just look good in a deck.

§ context

Allied Universal · Amazon account · 2019–2025.

Six years on a large, fast-moving logistics account. The job description is workforce optimization and KPI reporting. The real job is making sure thousands of staffing hours, scheduling decisions, and payroll line-items hold up to scrutiny week after week. Operations problems are stats problems in cheap clothes: variance, drift, and the cost of getting the wrong answer fast.

§ what I built

Dashboards & workflows.

  • KPI dashboards in Excel and Power BI tracking coverage, attendance, overtime, and payroll variance.
  • Scheduling & workforce models aligning headcount to demand patterns across shifts.
  • Payroll-accuracy audits with reconciliation workflows that catch errors before they ship.
  • Compliance & reporting for client-facing SLAs and internal performance reviews.
§ outcome

~80% fewer payroll discrepancies.

By restructuring the reconciliation workflow and introducing pre-submission validation checks, payroll discrepancies dropped by roughly eighty percent over the period I owned the process. The win wasn't a single clever model. It was treating the workflow itself as the thing under test, instrumenting it, and removing the steps where errors were quietly compounding.

§ why it matters

The stats version of ops work.

This is where I learned that a "data role" is mostly about owning the boring middle of the pipeline: the join keys, the validation rules, the edge cases nobody documented. The instinct to instrument before optimizing, and to design reports around the decision they support, came from here long before it showed up in a course syllabus.

Tooling.

Excel (advanced formulas, pivot, Power Query), Power BI, scheduling & workforce-management tools, payroll reconciliation, KPI reporting, stakeholder communication.
