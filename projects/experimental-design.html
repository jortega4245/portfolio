τ · treatment effects

Design thinking.

Potential outcomes, randomized inference, blocking, factorial design, power, and treatment effects — the part of statistics that asks how data should be generated before we ever model it.

blocks × assignmentT = treated
§ purpose

The estimand comes first.

This page is a home for the experimental-design side of my statistics work — the side that asks what claim do I want to make, and what assignment mechanism would let me make it? A lot of analysis goes sideways because the estimand was never stated; design thinking forces that question into the open.

§ core ideas

The objects I think with.

  • Potential outcomes: Yᵢ(1) and Yᵢ(0).
  • Average treatment effect: τ = E[Y(1) − Y(0)].
  • Complete randomization, blocked designs, and within-stratum balance.
  • Factorial designs, interaction effects, and main-effect identification.
  • Power, regret, tradeoffs, and design justification before data collection.
§ why I like it

Engineering, for statisticians.

Experimental design feels like the engineering side of statistics. It forces you to commit — to the assignment mechanism, to the estimand, to the variance you're willing to accept — before the data exists. Modeling without design is fitting; modeling after design is inference.
