Experimental Design Notes

A methods-centered page for potential outcomes, randomized inference, blocking, factorial design, power, and treatment effects.

Purpose

This page is a home for the experimental design side of my statistics work: the part that asks how data should be generated before we ever model it.

Core Ideas

  • Potential outcomes: Yᵢ(1) and Yᵢ(0).
  • Average treatment effects and estimator uncertainty.
  • Complete randomization, blocked designs, and grouped/block structures.
  • Power, regret, tradeoffs, and design justification.

Why I like it

Experimental design feels like the engineering side of statistics. It forces you to think about the assignment mechanism, the estimand, and what claims the data can actually support.

Future additions

  • Reading-speed experiment writeup.
  • Randomization inference simulator.
  • Power calculator examples.
  • Visual guide to blocking and factorial designs.

Stack

R, randomization-based inference, simulation, power analysis, ggplot2, reproducible design reports.
