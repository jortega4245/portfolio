Migration, Capital, and Mobility

An academic research pipeline focused on Latino immigrant geographic dispersion, longitudinal data analysis, and reproducible statistical modeling.

GitHub Profile

Problem

Migration and settlement patterns are shaped by mobility, capital, geography, and social structure. The research goal is to model these patterns using longitudinal data while preserving reproducibility and interpretability.

Method

The project uses R-based workflows for data cleaning, modeling, visualization, and fixed-effects regression. The work draws on longitudinal datasets including Add Health and PSID to study movement and settlement patterns over time.

Research Output

The project contributed to a conference paper and presentation, with ongoing work extending the model, refining reproducible scripts, and developing new visualizations for the next research stage.

What This Shows

This project demonstrates academic research discipline, reproducible coding, longitudinal data analysis, regression modeling, and the ability to communicate quantitative findings in a social science context.
