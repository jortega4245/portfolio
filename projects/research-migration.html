§ research context

Discovery Scholar · UC Berkeley.

As a Discovery Undergraduate Research Scholar at UC Berkeley, I support research on migration trends, geographic dispersion, capital, and mobility using longitudinal data and statistical modeling. The role is a window into how published quantitative social science actually gets produced — and the discipline it takes to keep it reproducible.

§ methods

What I do on the project.

  • Longitudinal data workflows in R — cleaning, reshaping, validating panel structure.
  • Fixed-effects regression for panel-style research questions and within-unit variation.
  • Reproducible scripts for cleaning, modeling, and visualization.
  • Research support for conference paper writing and presentation work.
§ research skill

Beyond running models.

This work strengthens my ability to move from messy raw data to a defensible empirical workflow with clear assumptions, clear model structure, and clear communication. Research code has to survive a co-author, a reviewer, and future-you reopening it in six months — that's a real engineering constraint, not a stylistic one.

§ output

Where it goes.

The research was connected to presentation work at the Pacific Sociological Association Annual Conference, with ongoing refinement of models, scripts, and visuals as the project moves toward publication-ready form.

§ stack

Tooling.

R, longitudinal data, fixed-effects regression, reproducible workflows, data cleaning, visualization, academic research writing.
