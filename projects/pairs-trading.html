Real-Time Pairs Trading

A quantitative strategy chamber for testing whether two assets share a stable spread, then acting when that spread deviates from its historical relationship.

Problem

Pairs trading depends on identifying relationships that are stable enough to trade. The challenge is separating a real mean-reverting spread from noise, unstable correlation, or temporary co-movement.

Method

The model evaluates candidate equity pairs through cointegration and ADF tests, then monitors the spread using z-score thresholds for entry and exit decisions. Backtests compare rules and thresholds to improve risk-adjusted performance.

Uncertainty

The academic version of the project adds parametric and non-parametric bootstrap resampling to estimate confidence intervals for mean-reversion half-life and expected spread return. This creates a more cautious signal system during volatile periods.

What This Shows

This project demonstrates statistical testing, trading signal design, backtesting discipline, threshold optimization, and risk-aware quantitative modeling.
